Should the Internet be Monitered and Regulated?

As the internet has become a central aspect of our lives, the question of how much it should be monitored and regulated has sparked significant debate. On one hand, there is a strong case for oversight to protect individuals from cybercrime, disinformation, and harmful content. On the other hand, many argue that too much regulation threatens privacy, innovation, and freedom of expression. Striking a balance is essential, with limited regulation aimed at protecting vulnerable users and public safety while avoiding the risks of overregulation. This discussion presents a balanced perspective that leans toward minimal regulation, with certain critical exceptions.
